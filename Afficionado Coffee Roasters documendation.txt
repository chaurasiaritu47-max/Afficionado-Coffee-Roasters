Chapter 2: Data Description
2.1 Dataset Description
The Afficionado Coffee Roaster dataset have a Transaction sheet of each products. There     are 12 columns in the dataset including the Revenue column which is created for the purpose to get the overall revenue getting from the product. Small description of each column as follows:
ColumnDescriptiontransaction_idA unique identifier assigned to each sales transaction.YearThe yearin which the transation took place(i.e. 2025)transaction_timeThe time of the day when the transaction occurred.transaction_qtyThe number of units of a product sold in a particular transaction.Store_idA unique identifier assigned to each store location.store_locationThe physical location or branch where the product was sold.product_idA unique identifier assigned to each product sold by the store.unit_priceThe price of a single unit of the product sold.product_categoryThe broad category to which the product belongs, such as coffee, tea, or bakery items.product _typeA more specific classification of the product within a category.product_detailThe detailed name or description of the product that is sold.Revenuecalculated column, to get total income generated from the sale of a product, calculated based on the quantity sold and the product price.

* There are various situations in which the Arithematic values are used. If the column values are numerical, we can substitute the mean or median for any missing values; for categorical values, we can use the mode.

4.2 KPIS
* Total Revenue: The total sales revenue from every transaction in the dataset is displayed by this KPI. It is computed by adding the revenue from all transactions and multiplying the quantity of goods sold by their unit price. It offers a comprehensive assessment of the company's financial performance.
* Product Sales Volume: This measure shows how many products were sold overall over the chosen time frame. It helps determine the total demand for goods and is computed by adding the transaction_qty values.
* Category Revenue Share: This KPI shows the portion of overall revenue that a certain product category contributes. It assists in determining which product categories account for the greatest share of sales and total revenue.
* Revenue Concentration Ratio: This measure indicates the proportion of total revenue that comes from the best-performing goods. It assists in determining if the company's revenue is spread across numerous goods or concentrated in a small number of important products.
* Product Revenue Contribution: This KPI indicates how much money each product makes in relation to total sales by calculating the average contribution of each product to total revenue.
* Product Efficiency Score: This measure assesses a product's effectiveness by taking into account both revenue creation and sales volume. It assists in identifying goods that are profitable and have strong demand.

Overall, the graph points to a well-balanced product line with steady high demand for a number of different things


The scatter plot shows a generally positive link between revenue and product popularity as determined by sales volume. The cluster of points on the right side of the image, where high sales volumes (approximately 4,000–4,500 units) correspond to greater revenues (about 10,000–21,000), makes it clear that revenue tends to rise along with sales volume. On the other hand, although there is some fluctuation reflecting differences in pricing or product value, products with lower sales volumes (below 500 units) generate comparatively lower revenue.
